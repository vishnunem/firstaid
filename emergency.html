EMERGENCY


Heart attack & Cardiac Arrest


First aid in case of a sudden heart attack

Dos:

  • Call emergency services:
    Dial the emergency number (e.g., 911) immediately to get professional help on the way.
  • Stay calm:
    Help the person sit down and try to keep them calm.
  • Loosen clothing:
    If possible, loosen any tight clothing around the person’s neck or chest.
  • Assist with medication:
    If the person has prescribed chest pain medication (e.g., nitroglycerin), help them take it as directed.
  • Use an automated external defibrillator (AED):
    If available, follow the instructions on the AED to provide potentially life-saving treatment.

Don’ts:

  • Drive or take public transport to the hospital:
    It is safer to wait for an ambulance, as the person’s condition may deteriorate en route.
  • Ignore the pain:
    Take any chest pain or discomfort seriously and seek immediate medical attention2.
  • Get anxious about time wasted:
    Trust that the paramedics will perform necessary tests and procedures to assess the situation

First aid in case of a sudden cardiac arrest

Dos:

  • Call 911 immediately to get emergency care quickly.
  • If more than one person is available, one should call 911 while the other tends to the person whose heart has stopped.
  • Start CPR immediately.
  • The American Heart Association recommends doing CPR with hard and fast chest compressions.
    1. Push hard and fast on the person’s chest — about 100 to 120 pushes a minute.
    2. If you’ve been trained in CPR, check the person’s airway.
    3. Then deliver rescue breaths after every 30 compressions.
    4. If you haven’t been trained, just continue chest compressions.
    5. Allow the chest to rise completely between compressions.
    6. Use an automated external defibrillator (AED) if one is available.
    7. Portable AEDs are available in many public places, including airports and shopping malls.
    8. They come with step-by-step voice instructions for their use and are programmed to allow a shock only when appropriate.

Don’ts:

  • Don’t wait for EMS to arrive.
  • Seconds matter. Start CPR immediately.
  • Don’t wait for someone else to step in. Start CPR even if it is not perfect.
  • Doing CPR gives the person their best chance.
Remember, sudden cardiac arrest is a serious and life-threatening medical emergency.
Immediate action with CPR and an AED can make a significant difference in saving a life




Road accident


First aid in case of any road accident

Dos:

  • Call 911 immediately to get emergency care quickly.
  • Check yourself and others for injuries right away.
  • If anyone is injured, call for medical assistance immediately.
  • Get all vehicles involved off the road if you are able to .
  • Take photos of any damage to your vehicle and other vehicles .
  • Trade contact and insurance information with other drivers .
  • Contact the authorities .

Don’ts:

  • Do not move your vehicle after an automobile accident unless necessary for safety or required by law .
  • Do not put yourself at risk of being further injured by standing or waiting in an area with traffic or other safety hazards .
  • Do not leave the scene of an accident until the police tell you it’s okay to do so .
Remember, it’s important to stay calm and collected in such situations.
If you witness a road accident, follow these dos and don’ts to ensure your safety and the safety of others involved.

BURNING SKIN INJURY FIRST AID


Fire accident


First aid in case of a fire accident:

  • Put out the fire or stop the person’s contact with hot liquid, steam, or other material.
  • Help the person “stop, drop, and roll” to smother flames.
  • Remove smoldering material from the person.
  • Remove hot or burned clothing. If clothing sticks to skin, cut or tear around it.
  • Stop the burning getting any worse by moving the casualty away from the source of heat.
  • Start cooling the burn as quickly as possible by running it under cool water for at least ten minutes or until the pain feels better.
  • Don’t use ice, creams or gels – they can damage tissues and increase risk of infection. Assess how bad the burn is.
  • Open a window, shout for help and stay where you are.
  • If someone’s clothes catch fire, make them immediately stop, drop and roll.
  • By dropping to the ground, wrapping tightly in a heavy fabric and rolling on the ground they will smother the flames.
  • Provide immediate first aid treatment for any burns.
  • These are some general guidelines for first aid in case of a fire accident.
  • However, it is always advisable to seek professional medical help as soon as possible.



Fuel fire


First aid for fuel fire accident

  • Do not use water to extinguish a fuel fire.
  • Water can spread the fire by splashing the burning fuel.
  • Do turn off the fuel source if possible.
  • Do smother the fire with a fire blanket or a heavy material like a coat or a rug.
  • Do not use a fire extinguisher unless you are trained to use it.
  • Do remove any clothing or jewelry that is not stuck to the skin.
  • Do not remove clothing or jewelry that is stuck to the skin.
  • Do cool the burn with cool, running water for at least 20 minutes.
  • Do not use ice, creams, or gels as they can damage tissues and increase the risk of infection.
  • Do cover the burn with a sterile dressing or a clean cloth.
  • Do seek medical attention as soon as possible.



Chemical hazard injury


First aid for chemical splits

  • Do not use water to extinguish a chemical fire.
  • Water can spread the fire by splashing the burning chemical.
  • Do remove any contaminated clothing or jewelry.
  • Do not touch the affected area with bare hands.
  • Do flush the affected area with water for at least 20 minutes.
  • Do not use a neutralizing agent unless you are trained to use it.
  • Do cover the affected area with a sterile dressing or a clean cloth.
  • Do seek medical attention as soon as possible.
